Energy Smart Metering

African utilities face challenges in improving their electricity infrastructure to enhance efficiency, reliability, and customer satisfaction. Smart metering technologies offer significant benefits, but the high costs and complexities associated with replacing existing meters create barriers to adoption. This paper examines the benefits, challenges, and implications of networking existing meters as a cost-effective and sustainable alternative to traditional smart meters in the African context.

Water Smart Metering

In KenyaAlthough exact data is hard to come by, Kenya has about 1.5 million household water connections supplied by 91 water service providers. In addition, water for household, industrial and agricultural use is accessed through an estimated 50,000 boreholes (of which 29,000 permits have been issued) and 4000 dams (of which less than 1000 have permits). At least half of these connections are not metered despite a legal framework that provides for all water extraction in the country to be monitored and fees paid to the Kenya Resources Authority. About half of the water service providers do not have billing systems; most of the other half have on-premises systems that do not generate online bills that are easily accessible to end consumers.
