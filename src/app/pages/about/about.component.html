About Pulse Networks

Pulses enable people and organisations to make informed decisions that improve their business operations and capabilities through real-time, real-world observations. At the heart of our organisation lies our belief that sensor observations paired with human insights, machine learning and Artifical Intelligence can unlock impact, meaning and collective intelligence. Enabling people and organisations to access this Artificial Intelligence in order to realise their potential is key to promote a prosperous future for all.

Addressing IoT Challenges for Emerging Markets

Current IoT solutions are prohibitively expensive and require specialist technical knowledge to implement. This is especially true for many small to medium scale environmental monitoring applications in the energy, water and agricultural sectors. The potential insights that could be gained from distributed sensor networks to inform decisions related to big national and global challenges such as climate change are thus not realised.

Our Unique Value Proposition

Pulse is positioning itself in the centre of an ecosystem that provides Data as a Service. We supply customers with hardware, software and technical expertise to build low cost LoRaWAN enabled sensor networks. Our innovative contribution lies in the provision of a trusted data access layer for connected devices, which powers platforms to access observations acquired by sensors. We see ourselves as a sensor network provider and intelligence platform enabler, with a mission to connect the world by making data acquisition for collective AI / ML seamless.

About Pulse Networks

Pulse Networks was founded by Joseph Wamicha in Cape Town in 2016 with the purpose of researching new methods of improving the efficiency and reliability of the utility sector in Africa. Pulse currently has a team of over 10 full-time and consultants and is entirely self-funded. The Pulse solution has been piloted in more than 20 locations across real estate, power and water utilities in Kenya and South Africa and is now ready for ultimate scale-up.
